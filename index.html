Games We Used to Play

Mille Fleurs has stood on this corner of St Clair since 1904. The games on the yard today, the stories being told inside, the kites being made and flown, the songs being sung, all of it belongs to a Trinidad that shaped every family here.

There is no fixed order. No wrong turn. Follow what calls you and stay as long as you like.

The Heritage Quest is at the heart of the afternoon. But there is more than enough to fill four hours without it. Move at your own pace. The yard is yours.

The Main Event

The Mille Fleurs Heritage Quest

✦ ✦ ✦

Somewhere on these grounds today, the stories of Mille Fleurs are waiting. The Quest will take you through parts of this house and yard that most people pass their whole lives without ever entering, guided by fragments of the history that made this building matter.

You will be asked to do things along the way. Some of them will feel strange. Do them anyway. You will leave with a card that belongs only to you.

How to join

Find the sign-up table on the yard facing the front of the house. Groups of any size are welcome. Sign up as early in the afternoon as you can. The Quest closes in the mid-afternoon and spots are limited.

Limited groups. Sign up early.
Hidden in Plain Sight

Prehistoric Visitors

✦ ✦ ✦

Scattered across the grounds of Mille Fleurs today are a few surprises. If you find one, scan what is inside. Something that walked this land long before this house was built is waiting to say hello.

How it works

Find an Easter egg on the grounds. Open it and scan the QR code inside with your phone camera. Point your phone at the yard and watch what appears. On your way out, drop the egg in the collection basket near the gate.

Who We Are

The National Trust of Trinidad and Tobago

✦ ✦ ✦

The National Trust of Trinidad and Tobago is a membership-based organisation established by Act of Parliament in 1991 to preserve and safeguard the built and natural heritage of Trinidad and Tobago. We are vested with the authority to identify, list, protect and promote the historic properties, archaeological sites and natural landscapes that form the physical legacy of our nation's past.

Mille Fleurs is one of the properties under our care. Events like today bring people into direct contact with that heritage and with the organisation that keeps it alive. We are glad you are here.
