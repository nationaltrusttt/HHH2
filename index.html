The Heritage Quest

Discover the real stories of Mille Fleurs

You are standing inside a piece of Trinidad and Tobago's history. Mille Fleurs has stood on this corner of St Clair since 1904. The Heritage Quest is a guided journey through the grounds and rooms of this house, following the stories of the people who shaped it.

Pick up your Quest Passport at the registration table near the fountain. Follow the clues to our Marshal Stops, get your passport stamped, and uncover parts of this property that most people pass their whole lives without ever entering.

Sign-up closes at 3:30 PM. Free and open to all families.

The AR Egg Hunt

Trinidad was once part of a vast Ice Age continent. Today, the animals that roamed that world have returned to Mille Fleurs.

1Search the grounds for hidden Easter eggs.
2Open an egg to find a QR code and a secret code word on the slip inside.
3Enter the code word below to unlock that animal.
4Tap the button, point your phone at the grounds, and watch what appears. Leave the egg exactly where you found it.
Found in Trinidad

Harlan's Ground Sloth

Paramylodon harlani

The size of a car and built like a tank. Its bones were pulled from Trinidad's own asphalt deposits, the same tar that still lies beneath this island today.

Found in Trinidad

Shasta Ground Sloth

Nothrotheriops shastensis

Smaller than Harlan's but unmistakable. Two different ground sloth species walked Trinidad at the same time, grazing a savanna that once stretched all the way to Venezuela.

Found in Trinidad

Teratorn

Teratornis merriami

A flying predator with a wingspan stretching up to four metres. Its fossils have been confirmed in Trinidad. Point your phone straight up at the sky to watch it soar.

From Trinidad's world

American Mastodon

Mammut americanum

Elephant-like creatures called gomphotheres walked Trinidad, confirmed from our asphalt deposits. The Mastodon was their close relative, roaming the same Ice Age continent this island was once part of.

From Trinidad's world

Saber Toothed Cat

Smilodon fatalis

The most iconic predator of the Ice Age. While its bones have not yet been found in Trinidad's tar seeps, this apex hunter ruled the ancient Americas that this island was once part of. Point your phone at the shadows.

From Trinidad's world

Columbian Mammoth

Mammuthus columbi

Thirteen feet tall with tusks longer than a car. Point your phone at the widest open space on the lawn to see just how massive a true mammoth was.

Find Your Way

The rooms in this building are named after the families who lived here.

  • A
    The Front Verandah
    This verandah has faced Maraval Road since 1904. It is doing what it was built to do.
    Bonnet Competition parade and judging. DJ. MC.
  • B
    Front yard, right side
    bmobile Games Yard and Maypole Dancing.
  • C
    Front yard, left side
    Picnic lawn, called games, Quest and Bonnet registration, Dairy Dairy.
  • D
    The Salvatori Room · ground floor
    Named after Joseph Salvatori, who bought this house in 1923. He hosted the most sought-after dinner parties on the island and counted dignitaries among his friends.
    Ministry of Culture and Community Development activation.
  • E
    The Camboulay Room · ground floor
    Named after the tradition that became the root of Carnival.
    Local Kite Making. Sign up at the door. Spots are limited.
  • F
    The Ballroom · upstairs
    The Salvatoris used this floor for family life and for entertaining guests with views over the Queen's Park Savannah.
    Chinese Embassy Kite Workshop. Sessions at 2:45 PM and 4:00 PM.
  • G
    The Prada Room · ground floor
    Named after Virginia and Enrique Prada. Virginia built this house in 1904 as a gift for her husband. She filled the yard with flowers and named it Mille Fleurs herself.
    Children's Storytelling. Sessions at 2:45 PM and 5:00 PM. No sign-up needed.
  • H
    The Mews · back of house
    The original stables. Now the working offices of the National Trust.
    Food vendors, face painting and horse riding on the grounds outside. Restrooms inside.
